How to screw learning with one simple belief

Jan 25 , 2014

This. Right here. Look at it.

It’s a graph showing the performance of children after they’ve been praised for their intelligence (“you must be smart”) versus their effort (“you must have put a lot of effort into that”), and then given a series of hard problems to try and solve. The kids who are taught that effort count increase in the number of problems they can solve. Those who are told that they’re smart show a frightening decrease.
